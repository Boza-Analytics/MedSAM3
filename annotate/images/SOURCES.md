# Tunneling Nanotube (TNT) Fluorescence Microscopy Image Sources

Real, openly-licensed fluorescence microscopy images of Tunneling Nanotubes (thin actin-rich
membrane tubes connecting two animal cells), collected for a training dataset.

**Total images: 50** — all REAL microscopy, all CC-BY / CC-BY 3.0 / CC-BY 4.0.
None duplicate the 5 images already in `test_images/tnt/`.

All images are figures (or single panels of figures) from open-access journal articles. Each is
licensed under a Creative Commons Attribution license, which permits reuse with attribution to the
original authors and source publication. Attribution = the article cited in the "Source page (DOI)"
column below.

## Notes

- All images are fluorescence/confocal/STED/light-sheet microscopy of TNTs or membrane nanotubes.
  A few figures are multi-panel composites; the dominant content is fluorescence TNT imaging.
- Older PLOS ONE papers (2011-2012: pone.0021283, 0029537, 0033093, 0033195, 0035766) carry the
  generic "Creative Commons Attribution License" (CC-BY 2.5/3.0). Current PLOS policy is CC-BY 4.0.
  Either way: free reuse with attribution.
- The Cell Death & Differentiation paper (cdd.2014.211) is CC-BY 3.0.
- Excluded: NIH author-manuscripts with unclear reuse, and any CC-BY-NC-ND material (one Nature
  Communications candidate was rejected for being NC-ND). eLife images pulled from the PMC
  open-access mirror (PMC11383530).
- These 50 are distinct from the 5 images already in `test_images/tnt/` (verified by md5).
